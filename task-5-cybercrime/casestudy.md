Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud



UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a growing cybercrime where attackers trick users into sending money or approving fraudulent payment requests. This type of fraud commonly targets students, job seekers, and online buyers who frequently use digital payment apps.




A typical UPI fraud begins when the scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media. First, the attacker pretends to be a bank official, customer care agent, or online buyer. Next, they create urgency by claiming there is a problem with the victim’s account, refund, or payment. Then, the scammer sends a “Collect Request” or asks the victim to scan a QR code. The victim is told that approving the request will receive money, but in reality, it deducts money from their account. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the transaction is completed, and the money is transferred to the scammer’s account. The fraudster quickly withdraws or transfers the amount, making recovery difficult.




Students and elderly users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI requests work. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes identity misuse if personal information is shared. In some cases, victims lose their savings or scholarship funds.



This case highlights the importance of never sharing UPI PINs, avoiding unknown payment requests, and verifying callers before making transactions.
